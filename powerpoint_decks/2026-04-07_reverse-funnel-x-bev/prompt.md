# Enquiry

You are a world-class presentation designer working at a top-tier agency (combination of McKinsey clarity, Apple minimalism, and Porsche brand precision).

Your task is to design 1–2 slides for a senior client pitch (Porsche Japan, executive audience). The topic is:

## "Reverse Funnel x BEV — From Demand to Decision"

## Objective

Create slides that feel premium, sharp, and intellectually powerful. The audience should immediately feel:
- This is built by a highly analytical mind
- This is actionable, not theoretical
- This is different from typical agency fluff

---

## Content to Visualise

Core idea:
We invert the funnel. Start from sales targets -> derive required leads -> sessions -> media spend -> channel allocation.
We use real observed performance (not assumptions), detect inefficiencies, and dynamically reallocate budget to maximise DCFS (leads).

Key pillars:
1. Start from Sales (Reverse Funnel logic)
2. Identify inefficiencies (conversion gaps, saturation, headroom)
3. Dynamic budget allocation (based on marginal returns)
4. Scale learnings globally (system, not campaigns)
5. Output = decision engine (not media plan)

---

## Slide Structure

Option A (preferred):
Slide 1 = Visual Story (hero slide)
Slide 2 = Supporting Logic (structured, minimal text)

---

## Slide 1 — Hero Visual

Goal: Visually explain the system in one glance

Design:
- Dark background (near black or deep graphite)
- Minimal text
- Clean, sharp typography (think Porsche / Apple keynote)
- Use a central diagram:

Flow:
`[ SALES TARGET ]`
`      ↓`
`[ REQUIRED LEADS (DCFS) ]`
`      ↓`
`[ SESSIONS ]`
`      ↓`
`[ MEDIA SPEND ]`
`      ↓`
`[ CHANNELS (Search, Inventory, Social) ]`

Then a feedback loop back from channels -> sales (closed loop optimisation)

Add 2 overlays:
- "Saturation curves" icon or subtle curve line
- "Dynamic reallocation" arrows between channels

Headline (top):
`FROM MEDIA PLANNING TO OUTCOME ENGINEERING`

Sub-headline:
`Reverse Funnel turns demand into a controllable system`

Bottom line (small but sharp):
`Every € is traceable to sales impact`

---

## Slide 2 — Supporting Logic

Layout:
Clean grid, 4–5 blocks max

Each block:
Short title (2–3 words)
1 sharp sentence (NO paragraphs)

Blocks:

1. START FROM SALES
   "Budget derived from targets, not guesses"

2. FIND THE GAP
   "Quantify inefficiencies across the funnel"

3. REALLOCATE
   "Shift spend to highest marginal return"

4. SCALE SYSTEM
   "Codified learnings across markets"

5. OUTPUT
   "Always-on decision engine"

On the right side:
Small visual element:
- mini chart (curve showing diminishing returns)
- or simple bar shift (budget moving between channels)

---

## Design Principles

- No clutter
- No long sentences
- Max 6–8 words per line
- Use whitespace aggressively
- Alignment must be perfect
- Font hierarchy very clear:
  - Headline large
  - Labels medium
  - Supporting text small

Color palette:
- Black / white / subtle grey
- Optional: one accent color (electric blue or Porsche green)

Icons:
- Simple, geometric, consistent style
- No stock icons

---

## Tone

- Confident, precise, technical
- No buzzwords like "leverage", "synergy"
- Sounds like an engineer explaining a system
