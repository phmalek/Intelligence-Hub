# Short Assessment: Reverse Funnel Light Budget Logic

## Draft Note

Following a review of the current Reverse Funnel Light workbook and [comment.md](/c:/Users/ali/repos/porsche/budget_setting/comment.md), I agree with the core concerns already raised.

At a high level, the current logic appears to be internally consistent as a narrow attribution-based model, but too restrictive for full-funnel budget setting. In particular, the model seems to anchor the reverse funnel on `Online Generated Sales` rather than the broader universe of sales influenced by media and website touchpoints. That means the upper-funnel requirement is likely being sized against too small a commercial base. I also agree with the concern that the current structure risks overstating lower-funnel need, because it appears to assume that all sessions required to deliver DCFS must be bought through always-on lower-funnel media, while upper-funnel media is separately costed through an impressions-based logic. This creates a real risk of overlap or double counting between upper- and lower-funnel budgets.

In addition to those concerns, I think one of the key assumptions used by PWC to derive the upper-funnel impression and cost pool needs to be challenged directly. From the material reviewed, it appears they are assigning fixed upper-funnel attribution weights at platform level, for example using assumptions such as `dv3 = 0.9`, `ttd = 0.9`, `fcb = 0.6`, `tik = 0.6`, `adw = 0.1`, and so on. This is directionally understandable as a first-pass heuristic, but it is too coarse to support a robust budget-setting recommendation. A platform is not the same thing as a funnel stage. The actual role of media depends on campaign objective, audience, placement, targeting approach, creative, and optimisation setup. A flat platform-level weight risks materially distorting the upper-funnel impression base, the cost base, and ultimately the recommended budget split.

For that reason, my initial recommendation would be to challenge the current approach on three points:

1. Clarify whether the model is intended to size only the attributable digital funnel, or to support full-funnel commercial budget setting.
2. Request transparency on how upper-funnel attribution weights have been assigned to each platform, and why this has been done at platform level rather than campaign or tactic level.
3. Request a clearer separation between sessions generated by upper-funnel activity and sessions that genuinely still need to be bought by lower-funnel always-on media.

At this stage, I would position this as an initial assessment rather than a final conclusion. To give a complete view, I would need access to the underlying notebooks and calculation logic used to produce the workbook outputs, especially the components behind the `RFL` calculations and the upper-funnel weighting assumptions. Once those notebooks are available, I can complete a fuller technical assessment and set out more precise recommendations on the logic, the assumptions, and the implications for future budget setting.
