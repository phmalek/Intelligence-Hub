REVERSE FUNNEL LIGHT CALCULATIONS 

 

In the workbook, the “RFL” tab calculates the ratios from sales back sessions, and looks at a cost per click for paid media to achieve the sessions. 

The “Share of MAL Budget” tab has calculations on how they get to upper funnel media budgets for future years based on 2025 figures. 

 

The basic assumption is that upper funnel media drives all sessions on the website (paid media + organic/direct sessions), while lower funnel media drives paid media sessions (and both are obviously relevant to the journey down the funnel to sales).  

My main concern is that the resulting estimate for upper funnel media is very low (16% of media budget) as compared to generally accepted media thinking. 

 

The logic for PWC’s upper funnel budget setting on the “Share of MAL Budget” tab is: 

 

They calculate the amount of 2025 impressions likely to be paid media related, by excluding anything clearly lower funnel (like search) – row 46  

They do the same for 2025 costs of these impressions – row 47 

They consider the amount of sessions directly attributable to paid media + organic/direct in 2025 – row 52 (note - they do use the total sessions target @Julien Eyraud (Omnicom)) 

They therefore know how many impressions they need for a session, as well as the cost of an impression 

They now factor in the target sessions for 2026, and can calculate the required impressions and what these will cost 

 

The only arguments I can make are: 

That they’re not accounting for non-digital media investment which is also driving sessions although the impressions are not captured. (Offline was 18.7% of 2025 Tier 1 investment). I’ll propose an upweight in line with this – they’ve already indicated that they agree with this 

I’ll also ask to see how they’ve weighted the impressions to exclude non-upper funnel ones. We served 731m impressions in GB in 2025, at a cost of about 4,5m. The calculations PWC use for upper	 funnel have 318m impressions and a cost of 1m. The split may be right, but I’d like to check that the ratio of spend:costs is right. 

 

I think there is also a logic error in using a reverse funnel based on Online Generated Sales to calculate upper funnel media requirements. 

 All of the RFL calculations are based on Online Generated Sales – 13.6% of total sales in 2025.  

The website is likely to be a touchpoint somewhere in the majority of journeys to a sale (I’m guessing around 70%?), but 86% of journeys clearly slip out of the attributable funnel at some point after becoming a session, and therefore aren’t OGS. 

(Note - within the OGS, 31% are directly attributed to paid media. This means that in total Porsche can attribute 4.2% of car sales to digital paid media). 

I think we can say that the sessions based methodology for upper funnel budget undervalues upper funnel media by only requiring it to deliver on sales which include sessions, whereas it needs to contribute towards all sales – so needs another factor of upweight for sales which don’t touch the website at all. 

 

Arguably, fundamentally the model also doesn’t understand the relationship between upper funnel media and lower funnel media (upper funnel media makes lower funnel media more effective). 

We could look to prove this out by comparing periods with different ratios of upper funnel media – but we’d need to account for the longer term effect of upper funnel media, and Porsche doesn’t have a lot of upper funnel media to use as data  = quite a complicated exercise. 

Lastly, there is a lower funnel calculation error that we don’t agree with. In the budget calculation, the always-on budget is inflated as it assumes that all sessions needed to deliver DCFS would be lower funnel.  

Many click throughs happen from upper funnel media, simply due to the volume of impressions delivered there, even without a call to action or tight targeting. We saw in the Medix Mix Light calculations last year. 

 

I also feel that we are double-counting paid media sessions – as the cost of media is in both the upper and the lower budget calculations. There should be a clear way of differentiating within the model.
